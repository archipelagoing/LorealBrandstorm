# 1. What L’Oréal ACTUALLY Wants

From the official mission document:

> Your mission is to craft the future of luxury fragrance using creativity, technology, science, and immersive experiences — not just products, but services and journeys. 

They explicitly want innovation across four core domains:

## Core Innovation Pillars

### 1. Product Craftsmanship

* New scent compositions
* New formats (solid, mist, wearable, etc.)
* New packaging and physical interfaces

### 2. Tech & Science Integration

* AI-driven personalization
* Connected devices
* Data-driven experiences
* Digital tools and generative AI

### 3. Transformative Experiences

* Immersive scent discovery journeys
* Emotional storytelling
* Multi-sensory retail and digital interactions

### 4. Community & Advocacy

* Social engagement
* Creator-driven discovery
* Cultural relevance
* Identity expression

---

## Strategic Translation

This means:

**They do NOT want just a new perfume.**

They want:

> A new interface between humans and scent.

Not fragrance as a static product —
but fragrance as a dynamic, interactive system.

# 2. The Biggest Real Market Problem (Your Entry Point)

From L’Oréal’s internal dupe audit:

* Fragrance dupes are growing extremely fast (+49%)
* The primary driver: same scent for lower price
* Consumers want:

  * Experimentation
  * Personalization
  * Identity expression 

This reveals a structural shift:

Consumers care less about brand ownership
and more about personal scent identity.

---

## Deeper Market Insight: What Consumers Actually Want

Luxury fragrance insights show consumers seek:

* Identity expression
* Emotional connection
* Memory association
* Personal uniqueness 

Exception-level fragrances succeed when they:

* Feel deeply personal
* Feel unique
* Have a strong emotional narrative

Luxury fragrance is shifting from:

**Product → Personal identity interface**

This is the white space.

This is where winning ideas live.
